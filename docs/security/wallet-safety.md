# Wallet Safety

**Your wallet is your gateway to LalaChain. Protecting it is entirely your responsibility.**

---

## The Golden Rules

1. **Never share your seed phrase** — Not with support, not with friends, not with "admins"
2. **Never store seed phrases digitally** — No screenshots, no cloud docs, no password managers
3. **Never enter your seed phrase on a website** — Legitimate services will NEVER ask for it
4. **Always verify addresses before sending** — Transactions are irreversible
5. **Start with small test transactions** — Verify the process before sending large amounts

---

## Seed Phrase Security

Your 24-word seed phrase is the master key to your entire wallet. Anyone who has it controls your funds.

### DO
- Write it on paper (multiple copies)
- Store copies in separate physical locations
- Use a metal backup for fire/water resistance
- Test recovery on a separate device before storing large amounts

### DON'T
- Take photos of it
- Store in Notes app, Google Docs, or email
- Store in a password manager (single point of failure)
- Share with anyone for any reason
- Type it into any website

## Best Practices

### Address Verification
- Always double-check the first 6 and last 6 characters
- Use address book/contacts for frequent recipients
- Beware of clipboard malware that swaps addresses

### Transaction Hygiene
- Review transaction details before signing
- Check amount AND denomination (ulala vs LALA)
- Verify the network (mainnet vs testnet)
- Confirm fees seem reasonable

### Device Security
- Keep your OS updated
- Use antivirus software
- Don't install browser extensions from unknown sources
- Use a dedicated device for large transactions
